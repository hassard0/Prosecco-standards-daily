# Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP)

**Status:** Draft | **Organization:** Universal Commerce Protocol

## Description
A solution for enabling generative AI agents to make payments on behalf of users securely and in a decentralized manner. It forms part of the agentic ecosystem alongside protocols like A2A and MCP to handle global commerce and payments.

## Latest Summary
The **Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP)** is an open-source standard co-developed by Google, Shopify, and other industry partners to facilitate "agentic commerce." It provides a standardized, secure communication layer between consumer-facing surfaces—such as AI agents, Gemini, and Google Search's AI Mode—and merchant business backends. The protocol's primary goal is to transform AI interactions into instant sales by enabling AI agents to discover products, negotiate terms, and execute transactions directly without requiring the user to navigate away from the conversational interface.

UCP addresses the fragmentation in digital commerce by establishing a common language for inventory, payments, and fulfillment. It is designed to be platform-agnostic, supporting various payment providers and e-commerce backends. For merchants, adopting UCP reduces the technical overhead of integrating with multiple AI surfaces individually. For consumers, it enables a seamless "direct buying" experience where an AI can handle the complexities of shopping workflows. The protocol includes specifications for secure data exchange, ensuring that sensitive transaction and customer information are handled according to modern privacy standards while maintaining a high-performance, low-latency connection required for real-time AI interactions.

Key components of the protocol include:
*   **Discovery & Negotiation:** Standardized methods for agents to query product availability and pricing.
*   **Direct Buying:** APIs that allow for secure checkout and payment processing within AI interfaces.
*   **Merchant Integration:** Tooling for platforms like Shopify and Google Merchant Center to expose their catalogs to UCP-compliant agents.

## What's New
The Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) has recently transitioned from a conceptual partnership to a concrete open-source standard. In January 2026, Google and Shopify released deep-dive technical documentation and blog posts outlining the protocol's role in "agentic commerce." The most significant recent update is the launch of UCP support for **Google AI Mode** and **Gemini**, allowing these AI surfaces to perform direct buying actions. 

New implementation guides have been published for merchants to begin integrating their backends. The protocol now emphasizes a secure, provider-agnostic payment layer, ensuring compatibility with major global payment processors. Additionally, the open-sourcing of the protocol on GitHub marks a move toward a community-governed standard, inviting third-party developers to build compatible consumer surfaces and business tools beyond the initial Google and Shopify ecosystem.

## Latest Summary
**ISO/IEC 23894:2023** is a foundational international standard providing specialized guidance on risk management for organizations developing, providing, or using Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems. It serves as an AI-specific extension of the generic risk management principles outlined in **ISO 31000:2018**.

The standard addresses the unique challenges posed by AI, such as lack of transparency (black-box models), data bias, model drift, and complex dependencies. It provides a structured framework for integrating risk management into an organization's AI lifecycle, covering governance, strategy, and operational processes. Key objectives include enhancing innovation through trust, ensuring ethical alignment, and mitigating potential harms to stakeholders and the environment. 

While it is closely linked to **ISO/IEC 42001** (AI Management System), ISO/IEC 23894 specifically focuses on the "how-to" of risk assessment and treatment within the AI domain. It is intended to be applicable to all types and sizes of organizations, regardless of their level of AI maturity. Typical users include AI developers, risk officers, and compliance teams looking to establish a robust risk-based approach to AI deployment.

## What's New
The publication of **ISO/IEC 23894:2023** represents a significant milestone in AI governance. As the first edition of this standard, it introduces a standardized terminology and process for AI risk management that aligns with global risk standards. Recent focus has shifted toward its implementation alongside the **ISO/IEC 42001** (AI Management System) standard, providing a comprehensive toolkit for organizations to meet emerging regulatory requirements, such as the EU AI Act. The guidance specifically expands on how to adapt the ISO 31000 risk management process—including identification, analysis, evaluation, and treatment—to the non-deterministic nature of AI systems.

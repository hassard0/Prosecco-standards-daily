# Intent Provenance Protocol (IPP) (IPP)

**Status:** Draft | **Organization:** IETF

## Description
A cryptographic infrastructure standard for carrying verified human intent through chains of autonomous AI agent actions using signed Intent Tokens. It ensures an unbroken, auditable lineage from a human principal to terminal actions executed by agents, incorporating properties like boundedness and non-repudiation.

## Latest Summary
The **Intent Provenance Protocol (IPP)** is a proposed cryptographic infrastructure standard designed to embed verified human intent into the operations of autonomous AI agents. As AI agents increasingly perform complex tasks—such as financial transactions or data access—across organizational boundaries, IPP aims to bridge the "trust gap" by ensuring every agentic action is tied to an unbroken, auditable lineage back to a human principal.

The protocol operates as a layer between cryptographic authentication and application logic. It introduces the **Intent Token**, a signed and tamper-evident data structure that carries specific authorizations and constraints. A core mechanism of IPP is the **Narrowing Invariant**, which dictates that any sub-agent or derived token must have a scope strictly less than or equal to its parent token, preventing "authorization creep" in autonomous hierarchies.

Key technical components include:
*   **Ed25519 Signatures & DIDs:** Used for identity and non-repudiation.
*   **Genesis Seal:** A cryptographic artifact linking every token to the original specification and its author, Amanda Haberkamp.
*   **Delegation Depth:** A field controlling how many levels of sub-agents can be spawned.
*   **Domain Taxonomy:** A hierarchical dot-notation (e.g., `financial.treasury`) for classifying intent domains.

IPP is designed to be framework-agnostic and decentralized, allowing any participant to verify token validity and lineage without a central authority. It specifically addresses legal and regulatory needs for liability attribution in systems where software acts on behalf of humans.

## What's New
The protocol has been introduced via its first Internet-Draft (`draft-haberkamp-ipp-00`). The most significant recent developments include the formal definition of the **Genesis Seal**, a unique cryptographic requirement that embeds the author's signature into every compliant token to ensure permanent provenance of the protocol itself. Additionally, the draft recently codified the **Narrowing Invariant** and the use of **Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs)** as the mandatory identity standard for both human principals and AI agents. The protocol is currently positioned in the "Security" area of the IETF as an independent submission.

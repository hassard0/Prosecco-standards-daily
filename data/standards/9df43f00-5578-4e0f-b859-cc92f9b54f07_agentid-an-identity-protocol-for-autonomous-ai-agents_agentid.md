# AgentID: An Identity Protocol for Autonomous AI Agents (AgentID)

**Status:** Draft | **Organization:** IETF

## Description
An open standard for establishing, verifying, and managing the identity of autonomous AI agents using Agent Identity Tokens (AIT). It builds on OAuth 2.0 and JWT to provide accountability, capability claims, and delegation chains for non-human autonomous actors.

## Latest Summary
The AgentID Protocol is a proposed open standard designed to establish a verifiable identity framework for autonomous AI agents. As AI agents increasingly perform tasks, interact with APIs, and chain operations independently of direct human intervention, the protocol addresses the critical lack of accountability and identity verification in non-human actors. AgentID building upon established standards like OAuth 2.0, OpenID Connect, and JSON Web Tokens (JWT) but adapts them for the high-speed, autonomous nature of AI agents. 

The core of the protocol is the **Agent Identity Token (AIT)**, a signed JWT that provides cryptographic proof of an agent's identity, its human or organizational owner, and its specific capabilities. A significant feature is the **Delegation Chain**, which allows for transparent tracking of authority from a primary principal through multiple sub-agents, incorporating "Scope Attenuation" to ensure that delegated agents cannot exceed the permissions of their predecessors. The protocol also introduces **Owner Verification Levels (0-3)** to communicate the rigor of identity checks performed on the entity accountable for the agent. This framework aims to mitigate concerns regarding unattributable abuse, impersonation, and rate-limit evasion in the AI ecosystem.

## What's New
The AgentID Protocol was officially introduced as an Internet-Draft (`draft-gudlab-agentid-protocol-00`) in March 2026. The most recent activity focuses on defining the **Agent Identity Token (AIT)** and its core claims, including `agent_id`, `owner_id`, and `verification_level`. Recent additions to the draft specify the requirement for **ES256** cryptographic signatures and the structure of **Delegation Chains** to support multi-agent workflows. The draft also outlines a new 4-tier categorization for owner verification (Levels 0-3) and establishes a "Scope Attenuation" principle to prevent privilege escalation during agent-to-agent delegation.

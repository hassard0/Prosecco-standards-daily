# WIMSE Applicability for AI Agents (WIMSE AI Agent Identity)

**Status:** Draft | **Organization:** IETF

## Description
This specification discusses the applicability of Workload Identity in Multi-Service Environments (WIMSE) to Agentic AI. It aims to establish independent identities and credential management mechanisms for AI agents.

## Latest Summary
The **WIMSE Applicability for AI Agents** standard (codified in `draft-ni-wimse-ai-agent-identity`) focuses on extending the Workload Identity in Multi-System Environments (WIMSE) framework to the specific needs of **Agentic AI**. As AI agents increasingly operate autonomously across diverse systems, they require independent identities and robust credential management mechanisms distinct from the human users who may have initiated them.

The specification aims to define how AI agents can be uniquely identified, how they acquire security credentials, and how these credentials are used to authenticate and authorize their actions across multi-system environments. Key topics include:
*   **Independent Identity:** Establishing a clear identity for AI agents to ensure non-repudiation and fine-grained access control.
*   **Credential Management:** Mechanims for issuing, renewing, and revoking credentials specifically for autonomous workloads.
*   **WIMSE Alignment:** Ensuring that AI agent identity patterns are compatible with broader WIMSE architectures, which traditionally handle microservices and remote workloads.

By providing a standardized way to handle AI agent identities, the draft seeks to mitigate security risks associated with autonomous AI actions and ensure interoperability between different AI platforms and the services they interact with.

## What's New
The latest update, version **-02** (released March 2025), further refines the discussion on how AI agents should be treated as independent workloads within the WIMSE framework. This version focuses on clarifying the "independent identities" requirement, ensuring that credentials managed for AI agents can survive cross-system hops while maintaining a link to the original execution context. The authors, Yuan Ni and Chunchi Peter Liu, have updated the document to better align with the core WIMSE Working Group's emerging patterns for workload identity. Recent activity has shifted toward defining concrete credential management mechanisms specifically for "Agentic AI" scenarios.

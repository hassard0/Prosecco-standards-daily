## Latest Summary
ISO/IEC TR 24029-1:2021 provides a critical architectural and procedural foundation for assessing the robustness of Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems. As a Technical Report (TR), it offers a comprehensive overview of the methods currently available to evaluate how AI systems handle perturbations, unexpected inputs, and environmental variations while maintaining their intended performance levels. 

The document specifically categorizes assessment methods into three primary pillars: statistical methods, formal methods, and empirical methods. It discusses how these techniques can be applied to verify and validate the robustness of various AI models, including neural networks. By establishing a common language and framework for robustness, the standard helps organizations identify potential vulnerabilities in AI deployments and select appropriate testing strategies. It serves as the introductory part of a multi-part series aimed at standardizing AI reliability and safety.

## What's New
The 2021 release of this Technical Report marked a significant step in AI regulation by defining a taxonomy for robustness assessment. It introduced a structured approach to categorize testing into **statistical** (probabilistic analysis), **formal** (mathematical proof of properties), and **empirical** (adversarial testing and benchmarking) methods. This foundation is currently being used to drive the development of subsequent parts in the 24029 series, which will move from high-level overviews to specific technical requirements and methodologies for deep learning robustness.

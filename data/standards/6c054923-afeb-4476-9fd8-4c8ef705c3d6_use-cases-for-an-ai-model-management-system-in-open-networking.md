## Latest Summary
The work concerning an **AI Model Management System (AI-MMS)** and its integration within open networking is primarily driven by the **Network Management Research Group (NMRG)** within the IRTF. The core objective is to move beyond running AI on high-performance servers toward a distributed architecture where AI inference services can run on commodity hardware, edge devices, and microcontrollers.

Key focus areas include the definition of configuration requirements for distributed AI inference, the identification of research challenges in coupling AI with traditional Network Management (NM), and the exploration of specific use cases such as **self-driving vehicles** and **network digital twins**. The research emphasizes the gap between existing NM problems—which often lack scalable or private solutions—and modern AI capabilities. This involves addressing computational costs, data privacy, and the efficient operation of networks to support heavy AI task distribution. Current efforts are also branching into **Agentic AI** for IP network operations and automated protocol testing assisted by AI models.

## What's New
Recent activity in the NMRG shows a significant pivot toward **Agentic AI** and **Automated Testing**. In March 2026, new drafts were introduced for **AI-Assisted Network Protocol Testing** (`draft-cui-nmrg-auto-test-01`) and updated use cases for Intent-Based Networking (IBN). There is also active coordination for side meetings at IETF 125 focused on **Agentic AI for IP Network Operation** and the **Interface to Intelligent Computing Function (I2ICF)**. These updates reflect a shift from theoretical AI deployment to practical, autonomous "agents" capable of managing network protocols and infrastructure with minimal human intervention.

# AGNTCY Identity Specification (AGNTCY)

**Status:** Emerging | **Organization:** AGNTCY

## Description
A specification for onboarding, creating, and verifying identities for AI Agents, Model Context Protocol (MCP) servers, and Multi-Agent Systems (MASs). It establishes a standard framework for identity management in autonomous agent ecosystems.

## Latest Summary
The **AGNTCY Identity Specification** is an open standard designed to provide a secure, decentralized identity framework specifically tailored for AI agents. As autonomous agents increasingly interact across different platforms and with human users, the specification addresses the critical need for a verifiable way to establish agent identity, ownership, and permissions without relying on central authorities.

The standard leverages decentralized identifiers (DIDs) and verifiable credentials to ensure that an agent's actions can be cryptographically linked to its controller. Key topics covered by the specification include:
- **Agent Self-Sovereignty**: Enabling agents to hold their own identifiers and present proofs of identity.
- **Controller Linking**: Defining how a human or organizational owner can prove authority over an AI agent.
- **Interoperability**: Ensuring that identities are recognizable across different agentic frameworks and autonomous ecosystems.
- **Security Protocols**: Establishing best practices for key management and rotation for automated systems.

By providing a structured markdown-based specification on GitHub, the project encourages community collaboration to build a trust layer for the "Agentic Web." This allows developers to build agents that can be trusted to perform financial transactions, access private data, or interact with other agents securely.

## What's New
The AGNTCY Identity Specification has recently moved into an active draft phase. The most significant recent activity includes the formalization of the **README** and initial architecture diagrams that outline the relationship between AI Agents and their Controllers. Key focus areas in the latest updates include the standardization of metadata fields for agents to declare their capabilities and the integration of DID-based authentication flows. The project is currently inviting developers from the AI and decentralized identity (DID) communities to provide feedback on the interoperability of the proposed credential formats.

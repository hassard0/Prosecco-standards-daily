# ISO/IEC 42001:2023 - Information technology — Artificial intelligence — Management system (ISO/IEC 42001)

**Status:** Backlog | **Organization:** ISO/IEC

## Description
This standard specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining and continually improving an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS).

## Latest Summary
**ISO/IEC 42001:2023** is the world's first international standard for an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). It provides a structured framework for organizations to develop, provide, or use AI systems responsibly and ethically. The standard follows the High-Level Structure (HLS) common to other ISO management standards (like ISO 9001 or ISO 27001), making it easier for organizations to integrate AI governance into their existing compliance portfolios.

Key focus areas of the standard include:
*   **Risk Management:** Establishing processes to identify and mitigate risks specific to AI, such as bias, lack of transparency, and safety concerns.
*   **Ethical Considerations:** Encouraging the responsible use of AI by addressing societal impacts.
*   **Operational Controls:** Implementing technical and administrative controls throughout the AI lifecycle, from data acquisition to deployment and monitoring.
*   **Continuous Improvement:** Ensuring that AI systems are regularly audited and updated to remain compliant and effective as technology evolves.

The standard is applicable to organizations of all sizes and sectors, whether they are developing their own AI models or deploying third-party AI solutions. By achieving certification, organizations can demonstrate to stakeholders, regulators, and customers that they manage AI technologies with transparency and accountability.

## What's New
Following its landmark release in late 2023, **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** has moved into the implementation and certification phase. As of early 2024, national standards bodies and accreditation firms have begun rolling out certification schemes based on this standard. 

Recent activity focuses on the harmonization of this standard with emerging global regulations, most notably the **EU AI Act**. Organizations are increasingly adopting ISO/IEC 42001 as a primary tool to demonstrate "presumption of conformity" with regulatory requirements. Additionally, ISO continues to update the administrative metadata and supporting documentation (reflected in the February 2024 portal updates) to facilitate global adoption and ease of access through the Online Browsing Platform (OBP).

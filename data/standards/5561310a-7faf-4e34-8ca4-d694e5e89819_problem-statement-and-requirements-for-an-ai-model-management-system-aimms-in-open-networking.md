**Status:** Backlog | **Organization:** IETF

## Description

## Latest Summary
The **AI Model Management System (AIMMS)** is a developing framework within the **Network Management Research Group (NMRG)** of the IRTF. It aims to address the challenges and requirements for managing AI models throughout their lifecycle in open networking environments. As AI transitions from high-performance servers to commodity hardware for distributed inference, the standard focuses on defining how networks should be configured to support distributed AI services.

Key areas of focus include:
*   **Problem Identification**: Highlighting gaps where traditional Network Management (NM) lacks solutions that AI could provide, and identifying the computational/privacy constraints of AI that networks must mitigate.
*   **Operational Requirements**: Outlining factors for client-to-cloud and client-to-edge AI service requests.
*   **Distributed Inference**: Developing guidelines for configuring systems to provide AI services in a distributed manner across microcontrollers and low-performance CPUs.
*   **Use Case Validation**: Exploring applications in self-driving vehicles and network digital twins.

The group is currently evaluating the research challenges involved in coupling AI with network management, including the efficiency of supporting distributed AI tasks and data privacy.

## What's New
Recent activities within the NMRG highlight a shift towards **Agentic AI** and **Automated Testing**. In March 2026, the group introduced *draft-cui-nmrg-auto-test-01*, which explores AI-assisted network protocol testing. Concurrently, there is significant momentum behind "Agentic AI for IP Network Operation," with dedicated side meetings held to define how autonomous agents interact with network infrastructure. The group is also progressing *draft-irtf-nmrg-ai-deploy-02*, which provides concrete considerations for distributed AI inference, and *draft-irtf-nmrg-ai-challenges-05*, a research product reflecting the group's consensus on the obstacles to integrating AI with network management.
